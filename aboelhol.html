The Great Sphinx

The Great Sphinx of Giza is one of the most famous and largest historical sculptures in the world, and it is an eternal symbol of ancient Egyptian civilization, located on the west bank of the Nile River.

The statue was carved from a single block of limestone and depicts a mythical creature with the head of a human (believed to be Pharaoh Khafre) and the body of a lion, symbolizing strength and wisdom. It measures about 73 meters in length and 20 meters in height, standing proudly in front of the pyramids for about 4,500 years, serving as the eternal guardian of the archaeological area.
